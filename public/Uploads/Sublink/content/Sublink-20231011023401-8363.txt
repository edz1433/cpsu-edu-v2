border-color: initial; border-image: initial; display: block;"></a></p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">Last December 6, 2021, the CPSU Research and Extension Offices in collaboration with the Philippine Fiber Industry Development Authority (PhilFIDA), represented by the PhilFIDA OIC Regional Director Evelyn Cagasan and Sir Nemspat Joevenet De Juan, the PhilFIDA Provincial Fiber Officer of Negros Occidental, commenced a five-day “Livelihood Training-Workshop on Basic Handloom Weaving” from December 6 - 10, 2021, to women farmers within the community at So. Lucay, Kabankalan City, Negros Occidental.</p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">A total of 30 attendees were present in the said activity with trainees coming from the organized farmer associations of the University, such as the CATAFA, MABAFA and LUBUSFA. The group was trained by Ma’am Mary Ann Montagon, one of the weavers of the Indag-an Primary Multi-purpose Cooperative in Miag-ao, Iloilo.</p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">“This engagement with PhilFIDA validates our serious commitment to take part in strengthening rural industrialization.
