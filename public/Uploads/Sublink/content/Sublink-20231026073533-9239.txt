Camingawan, Kabankalan City.&nbsp;</span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><img alt="" src="https://cpsu.edu.ph/files/housing-1.JPG" style="border-width: initial; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><span style="font-family: Rubik;">VP for Admin and Finance Dr. Marc Alexei Caesar Badajos stated that this opportunity is an outcome from the desire of the University to help the community. He stated that the realization of this initiative had undergone tedious process of planning to get the University BOR’s approval for the granting of the relocation site.&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><span style="font-family: Rubik;">“What the president [Dr. Aladino Moraca] wants is not to oust the settlers but to provide a better housing site for you. This will become a resettlement to all households in this community which will be provided with recreation center, as indicated in the plan. The said project will be similar to subdivisions wherein model houses are being prepared to have uniform building designs,” Dr. Badajos explained.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><span style="font-family: Rubik;">Kabankalan City Mayor Zayco lauded CPSU for realizing this project that will ensure safety and protection of the settlers within the community in terms of their land tenure. He further signified support by allotting a budget of 12 million for the road concreting project in the area to provide ease in transportation of the settlers especially in traversing to other barangays. This will likewise lift possible dangers brought about by the usual muddy and slippery condition of the road in the area.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><span style="font-family: Rubik;">Vice-Mayor Rivera also stated his commendations to CPSU seeing that shelter is one of the basic necessity of the people. Granting this project to the settlers in the community is a great initiative of the University and ensured everyone of the support of the local government for the success of the project.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><img alt="" src="https://cpsu.edu.ph/files/housing-4.JPG" style="border-width: initial; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; height: 233px; width: 350px;"><span style="font-family: Rubik;">&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><img alt="" src="https://cpsu.edu.ph/files/housing-5.JPG" style="border-width: initial; border-color: initial; border-image: initial; height: 233px; width: 350px;"><span style="font-family: Rubik;">&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><span style="font-family: Rubik;">Hon. Rene Dagunan, College of Criminal Justice Education Dean Sir Corold Romano, Campus Site Development and Beautification Director Carol Jane Parcon, along with the settlers within the community.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><span style="font-family: Rubik;">Immediately following the event was the launching and inauguration of the CPSU Green Technology and Community Livelihood center located just across the CPSU Main gate. Displayed here are CPSU’s own produced merchandise such as the Rainforest Coffee, Muscovado Sugar, essential oil-based products and sanitizers made from Citronella and lemon grass, along with other products processed by the University. Also displayed were farmers’ by-products and handicrafts, as well as ornamental plants for the green-hearted customers.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><span style="font-family: Rubik;">This project will not only promote and showcase the unique products of CPSU, but will also serve as means for farmers to sell their goods as additional livelihood opportunities for them.</span></p><p style="text-align: justify; margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><span style="font-family: Rubik;">The site benediction in the housing project as well as in the Green Technology and Community Livelihood&nbsp; was led by Rev.
