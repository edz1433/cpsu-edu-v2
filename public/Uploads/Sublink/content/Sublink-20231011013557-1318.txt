<p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">The University’s incessant goal of poverty alleviation in the region through reaching a wider scope of clientele has led to the forging of partnerships between CPSU and the Municipality of Valladolid last February 8, 2021 at the CPSU - Main campus, Kabankalan City, Negros Occidental.</p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">The said memorandum of agreement (MOA) stipulates the establishment of CPSU Technical Vocational Institution (TVI) offering on organic agriculture production at Purok Masagana, Brgy. Bagumbayan, Municipality of Valladolid, Negros Occidental.</p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">This pact is expected to promote and enhance the use of renewable resources, advanced technologies in organic production, post-harvest handling and processing, including the establishment of technical working group, among others, as effective mechanisms for the implementation of the Organic Agriculture Production (AOP).</p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">Dr. Aladino Moraca, CPSU president, stated that this convergence of CPSU and Valladolid in building initiatives and strategies to strengthen and hasten development in the region through education and agriculture technologies is part of the institution’s developmental framework to attain growth and progress in the entire</p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;"><img src="https://cpsu.edu.ph/files/vall-1.jpg" style="width: 408px;"><img src="https://cpsu.edu.ph/files/valla-2.jpg" style="width: 408px;"></p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">“Since the University is recognized as the bearer of agriculture in the Province of Negros Occidental, no doubt the interest of the LGU relies so much on CPSU to lead in developing the potentials of agriculture in the entire region. Hence, this activity aims to expedite the process through engaging in partnerships with LGUs. I believe in the commitment, and serious interest of the LGU to participate in the realization of this goal,” Moraca said.</p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">In response, Valladolid Municipality Mayor Enrique Miravalles expressed gratefulness to CPSU and stated that this partnership would spur great opportunities and substantive benefits to all Dolidnons, especially in the enhancement of their locally organized agriculture systems.</p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">Present to witness the said agreement were CPSU VP for Academic Affairs Dr. Fernando D. Abello and Valladolid Vice-Mayor Roel P. Yogore. With their team were TWG head Sharon Rose Lin Masa, TESDA Specialist II Jenie Notchite, TWG members Kerstien Trayco and Neo Abell Obligar, SB Larry Garde and Armando Alvarez and mayor’s office staff Mark Anthony Cabrillos.</p><p style="margin-right: 0px; margin-bottom: 10px; margin-left: 0px; color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: &quot;Roboto Slab&quot;, serif;">Dr. Abello presented and deliberated to them the current conditions, requirements, and expectations that are to be anticipated and prepared for the extension classes to be approved by the University Board of Regents (BOR).</p><p style="margin-right: 0px;
